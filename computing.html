Computing & Systems Development

About the course

Computers are part of our lives. Throughout the world there is a great demand for computer staff at all levels. A qualification in computing enhances the prospects for employment in this exciting and lucrative profession.

Choosing the right IT / Computing course could give you the key to a brighter future. The City College has a wide range of IT courses that will widen your skills and greatly improve your career and opportunities. Our IT and Computing courses will give you a variety of skills ranging from Computer Networking, Software Development or Computer Programming.

Entry Requirements

Applicants must be at least 18 years of age and possess either a level 3 qualification (or equivalent) or at least 4 GCSEs, one GCE A level with a good command of the English Language. Equivalent overseas qualifications are also accepted. Applicants over 21 years of age without formal qualifications with appropriate work experience may also be accepted.

All non-native English speakers or who have not studied the final two years of school in English will be asked to provide evidence of their proficiency in English Language equivalent to an overall IELTS score of 5.5 with no section scoring less than 5.00.

All applicants will need to sit a Literacy & Numeracy test and attend an interview as part of the selection process for Admission.

What can I do with my HND in Computing & Systems Development?

Once you have successfully completed your HND in Computing & Systems Development, you can continue to finish an undergraduate degree in a related field of your choice – this may depend on what areas of your HND course particularly interested you! Computing is such a diverse field with new frontiers developing all the time – you may be interested in specialising, in undertaking research or in becoming more involved in the practical application of your skills… Your HND is equivalent to the first two years of an undergraduate degree so you should be eligible for direct entry to the final year of a three year undergraduate degree in a related field. Different universities will have different entry requirements (in terms of the grade they require in your HND) so it is worth checking this as soon as you decide which university you might like to attend. If you are planning to fund this final year through the Student Finance England student loan scheme, you should be funded for this final year but you can check this with the Student Finance Advisor at the College if you have any concerns.

Alternatively, if you wish to go straight into work with your HND, you will find that you can apply for more senior or more specialist roles than before. Your HND will definitely be a stepping stone to starting your chosen career. Again, depending on the grades that you have achieved during your HND, you may even be able to apply for graduate positions and, of course, your work experience from before and during your studies will all count too!
